Why did I choose to join the Web Design & Web Programming team?

- Jeremy Brensinger

Hey, my name is Jeremy Brensinger. I’m currently a level 4 student in the Web Design & Web Programming lab at LCTI. I’ve been here for roughly 4 years, but what were the reasons why I joined this lab in the first place, and why did I stick around for the last few years?

Interests In Programming

The main reason I joined Web Design & Web Programming was because of my interest in programming, and wanting to be a video game developer from a young age. I’ve always been interested in how programs work, specifically video games, but other programs too. Before I started high school, my school allowed my class to take a trip to LCTI to see what it was like there. We went through all the labs that LCTI had to offer, and the lab that stood out the most was Web Design & Web Programming. The lab offered many different specialties such as Web Developer, Software Developer, Graphic Designer, and Multimedia Animator. There were amazing people in the lab and if I were to join, I would be able to follow my dreams of becoming a video game developer/programmer. After visiting LCTI, I decided to go to LCTI once I went to high school to try to get into Web Design & Web Programming, and after putting in a lot of hard work and effort, I got into the Web Design & Web Programming lab. You might ask, “Why would I join a lab when I can just learn how to program at home?” Well, when you have time taken out regular schooling, and have that time put into something you actually enjoy, you would most likely choose to go to LCTI. Not only that, but there are other reasons why I would want to learn at LCTI instead of at home:

Making New Connections

Another reason I joined Web Design & Web Programming was because I could attempt to make new connections with great people. When joining this lab I knew I would be meeting new people, and those people would most likely share the same interests as me. Usually when people share the same interests as me, I find it easier to connect with them, and become their friend. There were some people in the lab that shared career interests with me as well as personal interests (video games, anime, music, etc.). This made it a lot easier for me to have conversations with other people, and allow me to befriend them. I was happy to meet so many great people in this lab that I would most likely know for years to come.

Training For Professionality

The last reason why I joined Web Design & Web Programming was because I could train to be a professional. In this lab I learned how to become a professional in many ways. Some of these ways are utilizing soft skills to act professional, and creating professional items such as portfolios and resumes. I learned many soft skills such as teamwork, communication, problem solving, taking responsibility, leadership, adaptability, decisiveness, time management, and self motivation. These soft skills I’ve gained from this lab have without a doubt made me more professional and ready for the future. I was also able to learn how to create a resume and portfolio so I could showcase my skills, knowledge, and previous work/experiences. With making these items, I have gained experience on how to make them, and I can now use these in the future for jobs.
